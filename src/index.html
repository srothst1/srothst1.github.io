The Air Quality Index impacts the daily life of California residents.

It can determine which outdoor activities are safe on any given day. Recently, the quality of the air in California has been impacted by a spree of wildfires. The goal of this project is to use 3D and geospatial APIs to showcase wildfire and air quality data and help them understand how these metrics can impact their life.

How does the Air Quality Index (AQI) work?

Think of the AQI as a yardstick that runs from 0 to 500. The higher the AQI value, the greater the level of air pollution and the greater the health concern. For example, an AQI value of 50 or below represents good air quality, while an AQI value over 300 represents hazardous air quality. For each pollutant an AQI value of 100 generally corresponds to an ambient air concentration that equals the level of the short-term national ambient air quality standard for protection of public health. AQI values at or below 100 are generally thought of as satisfactory. When AQI values are above 100, air quality is unhealthy: at first for certain sensitive groups of people, then for everyone as AQI values get higher. The AQI is divided into six categories. Each category corresponds to a different level of health concern. Each category also has a specific color. The color makes it easy for people to quickly determine whether air quality is reaching unhealthy levels in their communities.

Air Pollutants and Health Effects

Poor air quality can irritate the eyes, nose, and throat, cause shortness of breath, aggravate asthma and other respiratory conditions, and affect the heart and cardiovascular system. Breathing polluted air for long periods of time can cause more serious problems.

CesiumJS

CesiumJS is an open-source JavaScript library for viewing and creating 3D globes and maps. Here, we are leveraging CesiumJS to view AQI readings at various location throughout California. Select a cloud to learn more about the data that you are viewing at that location. If you zoom in, you will notice that clouds are displayed above the terrain. The darkness of these clouds indicates the quality of the air in a given region. These clouds are an intuitive way of visualizing how poor air quality can impact our environment.

LAAirQualityBot
Created by @ian-r-rose

The LAAirQualityBot, created by @ian-r-rose, scrapes AQI and fire data in Southern California from a variety of sources. Then, it publishing data for Southern California Air Quality to a Twitter account called @LAAirQualityBot. This allows local communities to get live updates about fires in their area and AQI data.
